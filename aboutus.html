Why LiSuN

Trying to find a book in a library can be a cumbersome task. You go to the library and try to look for the book only to find that the book is not available at the moment. Most of the libraries do not have any online book search option, and even if they do, you have to log in to their local server to access that search option. Furthermore, what if there are multiple libraries in a region and the user wants to search all these libraries for a particular book remotely from his house. A few of the libraries have online platforms to serve the users, but these platforms are hosted on some server, and these servers are vulnerable to failures. We will be addressing all these problems in our Unified, Regional, Cloud-Based, and Serverless “Library Supervising Network.”

Features

  • Server-less cloud-based computing.
  • Access to all the Libraries in Thunder Bay.
  • View books collection from all the libraries.
  • Issue books from library of your choice.
  • Keep the record of different categories like; Books, Journals, Newspapers, Magazines, etc.
  • Keep the record of complete information of a book like; Book name, Author name, Publisher’s name, Date/ Year of publication.

What We Did

We made a server-less, AWS cloud-based Library Supervising Network where user can easily find the intended book across all the libraries in the city. This is possible because our system is centrally managed by the regional administrator who takes care of the availability of books in all libraries. Moreover, our application facilitates the library staff by reducing the manual work of keeping records of books. To increase the user-friendliness, we have built customized search options so that the user can lead to the perfect book in no time. As our web application is hosted on AWS, it is safe from unpredicted failure and thus always be online because of AWS’s characteristic of keeping redundant copies of single instance in different availability zones.
